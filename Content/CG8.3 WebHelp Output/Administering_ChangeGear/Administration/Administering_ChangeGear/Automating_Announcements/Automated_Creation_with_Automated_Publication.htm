Automated Creation with Automated Publication

ChangeGear can be configured via Business Policy Automations to create and publish announcements automatically based on conditions defined in the business rule. Information is derived from the RFC using variables. For example, if your company policy is to alert the IT and Help Desk staff of imminent major changes, you could create a business rule to announce the change to the staff automatically.

To create a Business Rule that automatically creates and publishes an Announcement:

  1. In the Administration module, navigate to Change Management > Business Policy Automation. The Business Rules list displays.

  2. In the Common Tasks menu, click New. The Business Rule DetailsBusiness Rule Details dialog box is displayed.

    • In the Name field, type a name for the Business Rule.

    • In the Description field, type a brief description of the Business Rule.

    • The Enable this rule check box is selected by default. If you do not want the rule to be active, clear the check box.

    • Specify how often you want ChangeGear to check for conditions matching your Business Rule.

    • In the MaxOccurs box, specify how many times you want the Business Rule to execute per item.

      Note: Click Execute to run the rule immediately.

  3. Click the ConditionsConditions tab.

    • In the Item Type list, select RFCs.

    • Use the lists to specify condition(s) that, when met, will trigger the Business Rule.

    • You can use the Test button to see which RFCs the Business Rule applies to.

  4. Click the Actions tab.

  5. Click New to create an action that executes when the Business Rule is triggered.

  6. Select Announce in the Create an Action dialog. The Announce detailsAnnounce details dialog will display.

    • Enter the Name of the action.

    • Enter a brief description of the action.

    • Set the Status to "Published" to have the announcement published to the Announcement Portal.

    • Click Edit to create the announcement.

      Note: To create an announcement with the same subject and body as the RFC, use the variables $(Parent.Summary) in the Subject field and $(Parent.Description) in the Body field.

    • Click Add to enter recipients if you want to e-mail the announcement. If you do not want to e-mail the announcement, leave this field blank.

    • Uncheck Post on Web Announcement Portal (when Announced) if you do not wish the announcement to display on the Web Announcement Portal.

    • Uncheck Enable if you do not want the action to be enabled at this time.

    • Click Ok in the Announce Details dialog.

  7. Click Ok in the Business Rule Details dialog.

According to the choices made in the images, your staff is notified automatically of each important change scheduled to be completed this week by e-mail, and the announcement shows on the Announcement Portal. This method sets up a rule which automatically publishes an announcement using templates and field data as the announcement body.

Other examples of how you can use this approach include:

  • Automatically announce approved RFCs on the Announcement Portal.

  • Automatically announce changes that will occur within the week to internal IT and Help Desk staff.
