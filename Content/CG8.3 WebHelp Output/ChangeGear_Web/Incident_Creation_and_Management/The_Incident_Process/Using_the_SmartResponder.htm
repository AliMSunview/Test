Using the SmartResponder

After you submit a ticket, ChangeGear can use the information in your ticket to help you find a resolution more quickly. The SmartResponder analyzes the content of new tickets in order to find similar tickets that have previously been worked on. It then searches the Knowledge Base for articles that may be relevant to your issue. ChangeGear then sends you an e-mail with links to the articles, information about your newly submitted ticket, and an estimated time for resolution.

Note: You may not have access to this feature depending on your ChangeGear configuration.

To use the SmartResponder:

  1. Verify that you can receive e-mail notifications from ChangeGear.

    Tip: You can configure your e-mail address by clicking on your User Profile (e.g. ) in the ChangeGear Web client.

  2. Open the Self-Service Portal and click Report an Incident.

    - OR -

    Navigate to the Incident tab using the ChangeGear Navigation menu and click New in the toolbar.

  3. Fill out and submit the Incident form.

  4. Open your e-mail client. You will receive an e-mail from ChangeGear regarding your newly created ticket. This e-mail contains two sections: the first section displays information about the ticket, and the second section provides suggested solutions.

    1. The Ticket section of the e-mail contains information about your ticket such as the ticket ID, summary, and estimated time to resolution based on the resolution time of similar tickets. Click on the ticket ID to open your ticket in the ChangeGear Web client.

    2. The Suggested Solutions section of the e-mail displays KB articles related to your submitted issue. Click on a KB ID to open the article in the ChangeGear Web client.

      Note: This section will not appear if the SmartResponder can't find relevant KB articles.

    3. Optionally, you can rate the helpfulness of the SmartResponder suggestions by clicking either the Yes or No buttons at the bottom of the e-mail.Clicking Yes returns a positive vote and improves the ranking of the KB articles as possible solutions to your problem. Clicking No brings you to Willow, where you can provide more information about your problem.

      Tip: For more information on Willow, please see the Using Willow topic in the End User Help.
