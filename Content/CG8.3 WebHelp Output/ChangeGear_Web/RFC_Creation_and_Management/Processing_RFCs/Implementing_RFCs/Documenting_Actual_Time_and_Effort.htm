Documenting Actual Time and Effort

You should not close a change request before you document the actual time and effort required for its implementation. Your change manager may want to compare actual time and effort against planned time and effort in the Analysis tab to help gauge the success of the implementation.

Note: You may want to count the time you spend documenting actual time and effort as the final task.

To document actual time and effort:

  1. Review each task in the Implementation tab to ensure that they provide an accurate account of the time and effort spent.

  2. Review the Actual group to ensure that it accurately reflects the following values:

    • Date and time that you began the first task

    • Date and time that you completed the final task

    • Total hours spent on all tasks

  3. Review the Total group to ensure it accurately reflects the following values:

    • Total effort in person hours for all tasks

    • Total labor cost for all tasks in person hours

  4. In the Resource Cost field, enter the total cost of any resources used for the implementation of the change request.

  5. Click Save.

  6. In the Comment box of the RFC Saved dialog box, type any notes related to the total costs of the change request.
