Planning Duration and Effort

Planning duration and effort can improve your understanding of the RFC’s cost and complexity. By estimating duration and effort, you can better decide whether to approve or reject an RFC. If you are sending the RFC to your Change Approval Board (CAB), the indicated duration and effort required help the board make an informed decision. Duration is the total number of hours required for the implementation of the RFC. Effort is the cost of the RFC in terms of person hours. For example, it may take only two hours to implement the RFC. The effort required, however, would amount to four person hours if the RFC required two technicians.

To plan the expected duration and effort of an RFC:

  1. In the RFC form, click the Analysis tab.

  2. In the Duration field, highlight the entire value 0 Hours.

  3. Type the expected number of hours needed for the RFC implementation.

  4. In the Effort field, highlight the entire value 0 Person-hours.

  5. Type the expected number of hours needed, multiplied by the number of people necessary for the RFC implementation.

  6. Save the RFC.
