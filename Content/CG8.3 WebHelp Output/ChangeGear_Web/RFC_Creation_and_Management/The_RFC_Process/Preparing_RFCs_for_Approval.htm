Preparing RFCs for Approval

To indicate that it requires review, each submitted RFC displays New in the Status list. At this stage of the change management life cycle, the review of the change proposal should be complete. Your next task is to approve the RFC or present it to designated approvers. If the RFC is minor or routine, you may approve it. If you have a significant or major RFC, it may require other approvals.

Some organizations form a body of approvers, known as the Change Advisory Board (CAB). The CAB is a group of people that advises the Change Manager in the assessment, prioritization and scheduling of Changes. This board is usually made up of representatives from all areas within the IT organization, the business, and possibly even third parties such as suppliers. They may also designate a sub-set of the Change Advisory Board to make decisions about high impact Emergency Changes. This group is called the Emergency Change Advisory Board (ECAB).

The CAB meets on a regularly scheduled basis to review the RFCs that are pending their Approval. The CAB members receive automated e-mails for each of these RFCs. Further, at the time of the meeting, the CAB can identify and review the RFCs requiring their attention by utilizing the Pending Approval view. The Pending Approval view displays all RFCs that are pending CAB approval.

To forward the change request to the CAB, you must change its status from New to Pending Approval. Upon the status change, ChangeGear automatically notifies approvers of the change request. While the change request is pending approval, the CAB may approve or reject it.

To prepare a change request for approval:

  1. Access the RFC.

  2. Confirm the RFC properly identifies the requester in the Requester field.

  3. Confirm the data fields contain the appropriate amount of information for the approvers to consider the change request.

    Tip: See Requesting More Information for complete instructions on requesting more information from the requester.

  4. Click the Analysis tab.

  5. Confirm the Analysis tab provides sufficient analysis for consideration of the change request.

    Tip: See Analyzing RFCs for complete instructions on setting requirements, planning time and effort, and estimating impact.

  6. Click the Approval tab.

  7. Confirm the Approval tab accurately lists the approvers that should consider the change request.

    Tip: See Adding Approvers and Removing Approvers for detailed instructions.

  8. In the Status list, select Pending Approval.

  9. Click Save.

  10. In the RFC Saved dialog box, provide any comments and click OK.
