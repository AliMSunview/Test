Overview of Analyzing RFCs

When a new RFC is submitted it is treated as a change proposal and goes to the New queue for review. Review of change proposals is critical to early identification of potential changes with significant cost, risk and impact to the business.

The Analysis tab supports the change proposal phase of the change management process. If you’re a reviewer or a change manager, you can use the Analysis tab of a change proposal to assess, expand, and refine the requester’s analysis. During submission, the requester may complete fields in the Analysis tab. For example, the requester may select or deselect check boxes in the Require group or complete the Alternative Solution(s) box.

Another part of reviewing change proposals is to forecast the cost of the RFC in terms of time, duration, and effort. The Plan group provides controls for estimating the RFC’s start, completion, duration, and effort. As you complete the fields of the Plan group, you can refine your ability to complete other fields. For example, as you complete the Plan group, you will probably gain a better idea of the RFC’s change category.

A significant portion of reviewing change proposals is to consider their risk and impact. The Analysis tab lets you account for the business risk and the potential impact of the change request. Again, you may have to revise the requester’s initial impact analysis. The Impact group provides the platform for expanding the initial impact analysis.
