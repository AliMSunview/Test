Before You Begin Using SLAs

Before entering SLAs into ChangeGear, IT should meet with their customers and other business partners to determine the service level and to confirm that the SLA meets business requirements. Consider the following questions:

  1. What conditional criteria are associated with applying a SLA to an Incident, Service Request or Change Request? For example, you may only want to offer an SLA on Incidents that have a Priority of High or Critical.

  2. When should an SLA be paused or reassigned? For example, you may want to pause the SLA timer while a ticket is waiting for approval. You may also have situations where a ticket was modified over the course of a Workflow and needs to be reevaluated to determine whether it still meets the criteria of the SLA.

  3. Are business hours or holidays a factor in computing SLA time?

  4. At what point in the SLA process should warning or escalation stages be triggered, and who should be notified?

  5. Who should be authorized to create or edit SLAs? Users who wish to use the SLA Manager must have the Administrator role.
