Interacting with SLAs in Tickets

When SLAs are enabled, some changes occur to both the grid and the ticket.

The Grid

After SLAs have been enabled, the SLA Stage column is added to the default ticket views. This column reflects the status of any tickets that are impacted by an SLA. The column is also color-coded for easier visibility. Tickets that are in the Warning stage are highlighted in yellow. Tickets that are in an Escalation stage are highlighted in red. Tickets that are in the SLA Exceeded stage (i.e., the SLA has expired) are also highlighted in red.

The Ticket

When you open a ticket, a new SLA tab displays. This tab displays all relevant SLA data including the SLA name, the total time frame of the SLA, the time remaining on the SLA, and a history of all actions performed on this ticket relating to the SLA. If the SLA was configured to respect business hours, the SLA Time Remaining and the SLA Total Time fields factor in the business hour and holiday settings defined in the ChangeGear Desktop Client.
