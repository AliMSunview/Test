Working with Global Willow Intents

Intents can be specific to a model, or shared among two or more models. You can review and edit global Intents (factory global intents along with any administrator-added global intents) that are available to all modules within Willow. You can also add global intents from this screen.

  1. Click Willow > Chatbot > Intents. ChangeGear displays the Intents (Willow) screen. From here, you can add new intents, select columns to show in the grid, filter the grid, and refresh the page. You can also drill down on individual Intents to edit or delete them.

  1. To edit an Intent, highlight its row, and click the pencil icon. ChangeGear opens the in-line edit row.


For the selected Intent, you can now see its settings and change them as needed. You can:

  • Rename the intent.
  • Add or change the intent's description.
  • See a list of every model that uses this intent, and selectively delete the intent from one or more of these models.
  • Activate or inactivate this intent in the Status field.
  • Assign or change the Action assigned.
  1. You can also delete the selected intent by clicking on the trashcan icon on the right. When you delete an intent, ChangeGear removes it from the model and also removes all of its associated Questions & Answers.
  2. Once you have saved your changes, you need to retrain the model in order for your changes to take effect. See Implementing Changes to a Model.

Adding a Global Willow Intent

  1. You can also add a new global intent to a chatbot model. To do this, click on the + at the top right of the Intents (Willow) screen.

ChangeGear opens the in-line edit row and enables you to enter the Name, Description, and assign Models, Status, and Action for the new intent.  Note: once you assign the intent to more than one model, it becomes a global intent.

ChangeGear automatically fills in the LastModifiedBy, and Modified date so these are read-only. When you are done with this row, click the save icon. 

  1. Once you have saved your changes, you need to train the model in order for your changes to take effect. See Implementing Changes to a Model.
