Problem Life Cycle

Regardless of your role in the problem management process, ChangeGear uses a work flow status bar to represent each of the process stages a problem report must go through before it is successfully resolved and closed. The work flow tracks the entire process from the submission of a problem report (New) throughout the life cycle until the problem is successfully closed (Closed).

Each problem report includes a record of the requester and the initial details of the problem. If you decide that the problem is more serious, you can promote it to an RFC.

Note: You may not have access to this ChangeGear feature depending on your permissions or ChangeGear edition.

The incident life cycle stages, (e.g., New, In-Progress, Pending Close, and Closed), are based on the actions you perform:

  • Submitting a Problem

  • Processing Problems

  • Problem Pending Close

  • Closed Problems

The work flow described in this topic about the Life Cycle of a Problem is based on a default process flow for a "typical" company. We understand your organization may have unique process needs and ChangeGear features a fully configurable process flow capability. To learn more about how ChangeGear can help you customize your work flow, please contact our Professional Services Group for more information.
