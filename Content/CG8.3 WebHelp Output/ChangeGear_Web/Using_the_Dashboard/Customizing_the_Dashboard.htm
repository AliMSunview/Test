Customizing the Dashboard

Depending on your persona, the Dashboard comes pre-populated with certain widgets. You can add, edit, delete, re-size, and arrange widgets on your personal Dashboard to your liking. You can also change the color scheme of the Dashboard by clicking Dark or Light button in the Dashboard menu bar. Any configurations made to the Dashboard will only apply to your personal Dashboard, not your persona's Dashboard.

To add a widget to the Dashboard:

  1. Navigate to the Dashboard module from the ChangeGear primary navigation menu.

  2. Depending on your privileges, you may need to select My Dashboard from the list of personas located in the Dashboard menu bar.

  3. Click Add Widget in the dashboard menu.

  4. Select a Standard or Predictive widget. A list of Widget Types appears. Standard widgets display historical data, while predictive widgets display anticipated future data. For more information on widget types, see the Dashboard Widgets section in Using the Dashboard.

  5. Select a Widget Type to add the widget to the workspace.

    Note: Each widget requires additional setup before they can be used. When added, they display "Setup Widget" as shown in the image below.

To edit a widget:

  1. Place your mouse cursor over the widget. A series of icons appears in the top-right corner of the widget.

  2. Press the Settings button to open the Settings dialog. Depending on the type of widget selected, different options appear in the dialog.

    • For chart-based widgets, (i.e. a pie chart, bar chart, line chart, donut chart, bubble chart, gauge or score card), select a Module and Chart from the drop-down lists.

    • For Custom HTML widgets, enter the HTML code that you want the widget to execute when it loads.

  3. Click Save to save your changes

    - OR -

    Click Close to close the dialog without saving.

To delete a widget:

  1. Place your mouse cursor over the widget. A series of icons appears in the top-right corner of the widget.

  2. Click the Delete button. A confirmation dialog asks you to confirm the widget's deletion.

  3. Click OK.

To resize a widget:

  1. Move your mouse cursor over the bottom right corner of the widget until you see a diagonal double arrow .

  2. Click and drag the widget to resize it.

To move a widget:

  1. Click and hold your cursor on the widget.

  2. Drag and drop the widget to the area where you want to place it.

To zoom in on a widget:

  1. Place your mouse cursor over the widget. A series of icons appears in the top-right corner of the widget.

  2. Click the Zoom In button.

  3. A window showing an enlarged view of the widget is displayed.

  4. Optionally, adjust the date range for this widget using the date range drop down menu in the upper left corner of the window. You can also fine tune the date range by using the date slider at the bottom of the window.

    Note: The date range you select in this window only takes affect while the window is open. It does not modify the global date range for the Dashboard nor does it change the widget's date range in the Dashboard.

Drilling into a Widget

You can click on a data point in a reporting widget (e.g. a section of a pie chart) to open a new window displaying a grid view of the data. You can further click on an item in the grid to display a form containing details about the item.

Note: Predictive widgets do not support drill downs.

Changing the Date Range

You can adjust the date range used to populate a widget in one of three ways:

  1. Select a predefined date range from the Dashboard menu bar. This applies the date range to every widget on the Dashboard.

  2. Use the Date Range Slider at the bottom of the page to specify a particular start and end date. The widget data is updated to reflect the date range specified.

    Note: The date slider updates a widget's date range via a variable assigned in the widget's report. This defaults to the creation date for incidents, problem reports, change requests, and Configuration Items (CIs).

  3. Zoom in on a widget and select a predefined date range, or use the widget's date range slider.
